The Outer Space Treaty, formally known as the Treaty on Principles Governing the Activities of States in the Exploration and Use of Outer Space, Including the Moon and Other Celestial Bodies, is a foundational document that governs activities in outer space. Ratified by over 110 countries, it establishes several key principles: outer space is free for exploration and use by all nations, no nation may claim sovereignty over any celestial body, weapons of mass destruction may not be placed in orbit or on celestial bodies, and nations are responsible for their space activities, including those of private entities.
